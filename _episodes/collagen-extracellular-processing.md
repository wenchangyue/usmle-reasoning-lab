quick_answer: >-
  The impaired step is **N-terminal propeptide removal** — the only **extracellular** step
  among the five options. After collagen is secreted, procollagen N-proteinase (**ADAMTS-2**)
  snips the terminal propeptides so the strands can pack into strong fibrils. Without it,
  collagen is still made and secreted but **never trimmed**, so fibrils stay loose — giving
  fragile, hyperextensible skin and easy bruising (**Ehlers-Danlos, dermatosparaxis type**).
  Only two collagen steps happen outside the cell: the **cut** (propeptidase) and the
  **cross-link** (lysyl oxidase, copper). Everything else — proline/lysine hydroxylation,
  glycosylation, C-terminal disulfide alignment, triple-helix formation — is intracellular.
faq:
  - q: Why is the answer N-terminal propeptide removal?
    a: >-
      The stem says the defect is in processing AFTER the cell secretes the collagen — that is,
      extracellular. Of the five options, only propeptide removal happens outside the cell. So the
      compartment word alone selects it, before you name any disease.
  - q: How do you tell intracellular from extracellular collagen steps?
    a: >-
      Only two steps are extracellular: cleaving the propeptides (procollagen N-proteinase /
      ADAMTS-2 and C-proteinase / BMP-1), which turns procollagen into tropocollagen, and
      cross-linking (lysyl oxidase, copper-dependent). Chain synthesis, proline/lysine
      hydroxylation, hydroxylysine glycosylation, and triple-helix formation are all intracellular.
  - q: Why isn't it proline hydroxylation (choice D)?
    a: >-
      Proline hydroxylation is intracellular and needs vitamin C; losing it is scurvy, not this
      picture. It fails the one filter the stem gave you — the defect here is extracellular.
  - q: What disease is this, and which enzyme is missing?
    a: >-
      Ehlers-Danlos syndrome, dermatosparaxis type, caused by deficiency of procollagen
      N-proteinase (ADAMTS-2). The propeptides never come off, so collagen units cannot pack into
      strong fibrils — hence fragile, stretchy skin that tears around sutures, easy bruising, and
      hernias.
  - q: If a question only names the enzyme, how do you find the disease?
    a: >-
      Read the suffix. "-hydroxylase" adds a hydroxyl group (inside, needs vitamin C → scurvy).
      "-proteinase" cuts the propeptide (outside → Ehlers-Danlos, dermatosparaxis). "-oxidase" is
      lysyl oxidase, which cross-links using copper (outside → Menkes). A memory hook for the last
      one: Oxidase = Outside + cOpper.
