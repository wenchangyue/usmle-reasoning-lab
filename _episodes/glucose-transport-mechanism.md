**Clara:** Good, that's the distinction to nail. Telling glucose from fructose is structural specificity: those are different molecules, built differently. What the stem shows is stronger. **Yue:** And why is that sharper claim the one that proves a protein?

**Clara:** Because a plain lipid membrane, just oily fat, cannot tell a left hand from a right hand. **Yue:** Like a glove.

**Yue:** But physically, how does one little pocket tell two mirror images apart?

**Clara:** The clean textbook picture is three-point attachment. The binding pocket grips the sugar at three points at once. For D-glucose, all three contacts line up and it locks in. **Clara:** It flattens. That plateau has a name, the maximal rate, V-max. A ceiling means there's a fixed number of carriers. Each one can only cycle so fast.
