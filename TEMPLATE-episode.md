faq:
  - q: What enzyme is deficient in hereditary fructose intolerance?
    a: >-
      Aldolase B. It normally splits fructose-1-phosphate; without it, F1P
      accumulates, traps inorganic phosphate, and blocks glycogenolysis and
      gluconeogenesis — causing hypoglycemia after fructose.
  - q: How is it different from galactosemia?
    a: >-
      Both are inborn errors that show up at a dietary change, but galactosemia
      appears with **milk/lactose** (deficient GALT) whereas HFI appears with
      **fruit/sucrose/fructose** (deficient aldolase B).
